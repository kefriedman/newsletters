Google DeepMind released Gemini 3.1 Flash TTS, featuring granular audio tags that give developers precise control over AI-generated speech. This breakthrough enables more expressive and contextually appropriate voice synthesis for applications ranging from audiobooks to virtual assistants. For practitioners, this means significantly improved user experiences in voice-based applications with fine-tuned emotional expression and speaking styles.

Research Worth Knowing

Being Polite to AI Models Doesn't Actually Help

Researchers tested whether politeness affects LLM performance across multiple languages and models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini, etc.) and found no consistent benefit from polite prompts. This debunks the popular notion that saying "please" improves AI responses, saving practitioners from unnecessary prompt engineering efforts. Read the paper

New Method Makes AI Uncertainty Estimates More Reliable

Standard confidence scores from language models often break down in real-world deployment. This work introduces a technique using internal model representations instead of surface-level probabilities to better predict when an AI might be wrong. This could significantly improve reliability for high-stakes applications where knowing when to trust AI output is crucial. Read the paper

Efficient Continual Learning Method Prevents AI Forgetting

JumpLoRA introduces a sparse adapter approach that lets large language models learn new tasks without forgetting previous ones. This addresses a major practical challenge in deploying AI systems that need to continuously learn and adapt while maintaining existing capabilities. Read the paper
