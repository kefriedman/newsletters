Research Worth Knowing

LLMs Are Terrible at Knowing When to Say "I Don't Know"

Researchers found that large language models consistently give confident answers even when they should abstain from responding. They developed a new metric called BAS that evaluates whether AI systems can properly gauge their own confidence and stay quiet when uncertain. This matters because overconfident AI can be dangerous in high-stakes applications like healthcare or finance.

Read the paper

AI Citation Links Are Often Completely Fake

A systematic study of commercial LLMs and research agents found that 3-13% of citation URLs they provide are completely hallucinated—they never existed at all. Another 5-18% lead to irrelevant content. This is a critical issue for AI tools being used in research and professional writing, where accurate citations are essential.

Read the paper

Why Scaling Vision-Language-Action Models Hits a Wall

Researchers discovered that upgrading the vision component in robotics AI doesn't improve performance as expected when actions are represented as discrete tokens. The bottleneck shifts to action representation, limiting the benefits of better vision. This explains why many robotics AI scaling efforts plateau and suggests focusing on continuous action representations instead.
